# Mission

## Why Unfortunately Exists

The hiring process is broken. Everyone knows it. Nobody fixes it.

Candidates spend hours crafting applications for positions that were already filled internally. They optimize resumes for algorithms that discard them before any human reads them. They rehearse answers to questions designed not to understand them but to filter them. They receive rejection emails written by no one.

Employers, meanwhile, have developed an entire vocabulary for avoiding accountability. "We'll keep your resume on file." "We're looking for a culture fit." "The role has been put on hold." These phrases exist to end conversations without honesty.

Unfortunately exists because this imbalance deserved a response.

---

## Why Satire

Satire is not decoration. It is a diagnostic tool.

When a system is broken in ways that are too normalized to criticize directly, satire makes the dysfunction visible. It reframes what everyone experiences but nobody names.

Unfortunately uses satire to simulate the employer side of the hiring process — the automated rejections, the performative enthusiasm, the corporate non-answers. By letting candidates experience the machinery from the inside, we make it legible.

The goal is not cruelty. It is clarity.

We punch upward. We do not mock candidates. We simulate the systems that diminish them.

---

## Why Honesty Matters

Most products in this space trade in false hope. They promise that the right resume, the right keyword density, the right LinkedIn headline will unlock the door.

We don't make that promise.

We tell the truth about how hiring actually works. We document what companies actually say. We build simulations based on real patterns, not idealized ones.

Honesty is not a strategy. It is a prerequisite.

If we fabricate employer behavior to make our product more entertaining, we become part of the same deception we are criticizing.

---

## Why Transparency Matters

We disclose when content is AI-generated. We attribute sources. We do not hide our methods.

Candidates trust us with their frustration, their time, and sometimes their job search. That trust requires transparency about what we are and what we are not.

We are a satire product. We are not a hiring platform. We do not place candidates. We do not have inside access to employer decisions.

We are honest about all of this.

---

## The Long-Term Vision

Unfortunately, at scale, becomes a record.

A record of how companies treat candidates. A record of which interview patterns are dehumanizing. A record of what corporate language actually means when translated.

Over time, this record exerts pressure. Companies that appear in Unfortunately's simulations have an incentive to behave better in the real world — not because we expose them, but because we make the gap between their self-image and their practice visible.

The long-term vision is a hiring ecosystem that is more honest because a generation of candidates practiced reading the room.
