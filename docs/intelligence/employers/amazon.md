Culture:
Amazon operates on 16 Leadership Principles (LPs) that govern all hiring decisions, performance reviews, and daily work. Key LPs include Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Frugality, and Dive Deep. The culture is demanding and process-heavy. Amazon enforced a controversial 5-day return-to-office mandate effective January 2, 2025, impacting ~350,000 corporate employees. An internal Blind survey found 91% of employees were dissatisfied with the RTO policy. 48% of affected employees applied to other jobs within weeks of the announcement. The company operates on a "Day 1" philosophy — meaning always acting like a startup, never coasting.

Reputation:
Mixed. Respected for scale, compensation, and career acceleration. Criticized for high attrition, performance-managed exits ("Rank and Yank" culture), and intense pressure. Glassdoor overall interview positivity rate: 57.6%. RTO mandate damaged employer brand significantly in 2024-2025 and accelerated brain drain of senior engineers. Theory widely discussed: RTO was designed to reduce headcount without formal layoffs. Stanford economist Nicholas Bloom cited this publicly.

Interview Style:
Structured, behavioral-heavy, multi-round loop. Standard process:
1. Online Assessment (OA): 2 DSA problems (Medium-Hard) + behavioral section
2. Phone screen: 30-45 min with recruiter or hiring manager
3. Interview loop: 4-6 rounds, 45-60 min each
4. Bar Raiser: An objective interviewer from outside the hiring team ensures standards

Every behavioral question maps to a specific Leadership Principle. Interviewers are each assigned 2 LPs and ask 2-3 questions per LP using STAR format. Candidates must prepare unique STAR stories — Amazon tracks story repetition across interviewers. Process takes 4-8 weeks end-to-end. Feedback typically within 2 business days post-phone screen; 5 business days post-loop.

Common Criticism:
- Rejection without meaningful feedback ("improve your problem-solving" with no specifics)
- Bar Raiser process feels arbitrary — candidates pass 3 rounds and fail on a surprise 4th
- Process favors those who memorize LP frameworks over actual job capability
- RTO mandate seen as hypocritical given Amazon's remote-work growth during COVID
- 45% of employees are not in the same office as their direct manager, undermining stated RTO rationale
- High interview volume with little transparency on decision criteria
- Ghost listings reported by candidates

Employee Praise:
- Genuinely smart colleagues
- Career acceleration: fast promotions for high performers
- Scale of problems is unmatched
- Strong compensation (especially TC for senior engineers)
- Encourages innovation and big thinking
- AWS experience is a valuable career credential

Leadership Principles (16 total, public):
Customer Obsession, Ownership, Invent and Simplify, Are Right A Lot, Learn and Be Curious, Hire and Develop the Best, Insist on the Highest Standards, Think Big, Bias for Action, Frugality, Earn Trust, Dive Deep, Have Backbone / Disagree and Commit, Deliver Results, Strive to be Earth's Best Employer, Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility.
Source: https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles

Candidate FAQs:
- Q: How long does Amazon's hiring process take? A: 4-8 weeks typically.
- Q: What is a Bar Raiser? A: An interviewer from outside your hiring team who holds veto power and ensures hiring bar is maintained.
- Q: Do I need to know all 16 LPs? A: Yes. Every behavioral question maps to a LP. Know examples for each.
- Q: Will I get feedback if rejected? A: Rarely. Amazon does not provide detailed rejection feedback.
- Q: Is the 5-day RTO still in effect? A: Yes, as of 2025-2026. No reversal announced.
