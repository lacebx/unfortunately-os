Culture:
Meta operates under Mark Zuckerberg's directorship with a culture of "Move Fast and Break Things" — now evolved to "Move Fast with Stable Infrastructure." The 2023 "Year of Efficiency" resulted in two major rounds of layoffs: 11,000 in November 2022 and 10,000 in March 2023, totaling ~21,000 employees (roughly 25% of the workforce). Recruiting teams were disproportionately impacted. The remaining culture is described as leaner, more demanding, and more AI-focused. 2024-2025 saw Meta significantly accelerate hiring in AI, Reality Labs infrastructure, and Llama AI research. Meta's values are: Move Fast, Be Bold, Focus on Long-Term Impact, Be Open, Build Social Value.

Reputation:
Mixed-to-positive among engineers who remain. Pre-layoff Meta was known as an elite destination with strong culture and compensation. Post-layoff, morale and employer brand suffered considerably. Glassdoor ratings reflect a company in transition. Interview process rated as highly variable by candidates — Blind users note the process is "very manual" and candidate experience depends heavily on which recruiter and team you deal with. Metaverse direction is a source of ongoing uncertainty about company direction among candidates.

Interview Style:
Technical-focused, efficient compared to Google. Process:
1. Recruiter screen
2. Technical phone screen (45 min coding)
3. Onsite/virtual loop: 4-5 rounds
   - 2 coding rounds (LeetCode Medium-Hard, focus on product/system)
   - 1 system design round
   - 1 behavioral round ("Jedi" — behavioral with structured stories)
   - 1 product sense round (for PM/non-eng roles)
4. Hiring decision (typically within 1-2 weeks of loop)

Meta does not have a formal Bar Raiser equivalent. Hiring manager has more direct influence. Process is more direct than Google, typically taking 4-8 weeks. Post-layoff, the process became even more manual as recruiting headcount was reduced.

Common Criticism:
- Metaverse bet creates uncertainty about company direction and long-term stability
- Highly manual hiring process leads to inconsistent candidate experience
- Reality Labs (VR/AR) division has burned billions with unclear ROI — candidates question whether to join

Candidate FAQs:
- Q: How long does Meta's hiring process take? A: 4-8 weeks typically, faster than Google.
- Q: What is the "Jedi" round? A: Meta's behavioral interview — expects structured STAR-format stories about collaboration, conflict, and leadership.
- Q: Does Meta still have layoffs? A: Post-2023, Meta is actively hiring in AI and infrastructure but overall headcount remains leaner. Targeted layoffs may still occur by division.
- Q: Is Reality Labs worth joining? A: Highly debated. Strong engineering problems, but uncertain commercial future.
- Q: What coding difficulty does Meta use? A: LeetCode Medium-Hard. Meta favors product-aware system design.
