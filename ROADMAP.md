# Roadmap

This file tracks the long-term product direction for Unfortunately. It is maintained by the founder and updated when strategic priorities shift.

All active development belongs in `/execution/`. This file describes direction, not tasks.

---

## Current Phase: Foundation

Focus: Build the core product experience. Get the interview simulation right. Establish the company voice. Find the first 100 users who truly get it.

---

## Phase 1 — Core Product (Now)

- [ ] Employer simulation engine (core interviews)
- [ ] Company profiles with interview style notes
- [ ] Rejection email generator
- [ ] Share results functionality
- [ ] Basic user accounts
- [ ] Mobile-responsive layout
- [ ] Initial company library (20+ companies)

---

## Phase 2 — Community Signal

- [ ] Community submissions (suggest a company, report an experience)
- [ ] User-submitted rejection emails (opt-in)
- [ ] "Was this realistic?" feedback loop
- [ ] Leaderboard or streak mechanic (if it fits the tone)
- [ ] Interview difficulty ratings
- [ ] Shareable rejection certificates

---

## Phase 3 — Intelligence Layer

- [ ] Company-specific interview trend tracking
- [ ] Hiring freeze / hiring surge signals
- [ ] "Companies ghosting candidates" reports
- [ ] Interview question database (crowd-sourced)
- [ ] Employer reputation index

---

## Phase 4 — Platform

- [ ] API for third-party integrations
- [ ] Embeddable interview simulators
- [ ] Newsletter / digest product
- [ ] Employer response portal (opt-in)
- [ ] Premium features (TBD based on user feedback)

---

## Long-Term Bets

These are directional hypotheses, not commitments.

- Unfortunately becomes the Glassdoor of interview experiences
- The rejection email format becomes a cultural artifact
- Companies improve their hiring processes because they don't want to appear in our simulations
- A generation of job seekers learns to read corporate language through Unfortunately

---

## What We Will Not Build

- Resume optimization tools (we don't traffic in false hope)
- Employer-facing recruitment products (conflict of interest)
- Fake job listings
- Paid placement or sponsored content
- Features that require us to lie to users
